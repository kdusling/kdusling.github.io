Instructions:
  1. Choose the magnitude of charge (in Coulombs) from the "Select Charge" drop down menu.
  2. Place the charge on the canvas by clicking on the desired location for the charge.
  3. Continue to place charges on the canvas as desired. The sign and magnitude of each subsequent charge can be chosen from the "Select Charge" menu.
  4. When your are satisfied with the configuration of charges, set the number of field lines that will emanate from a 1 Coulomb charge. Also choose the relative precision of the calculation. A precision of -2 means that the filed lines will have a relative error of one part in 100.
  5. Select "Draw Field Lines" to compute and display the electric field lines.
  6. Additionally, one can draw the electric field vector at a given point by first selecting "Draw Field Vector" and then clicking on the desired location on the canvas.
  7. The equipotential surface passing through a given point can be computed by selecting "Draw equipotential" and then clicking on the desired location on the canvas.
  8. Choosing "Reverse Charges" will flip the sign of all charges on the canvas.
  9. There is also the option to load a preset configuration of charges. Selecting one of these presets will clear your current work.
  10. The print command will open a new window containing a jpeg image of the canvas. This jpeg can then be saved locally to your computer.
  11. Note that the caveats discussed in the article Electric field line diagrams don't work apply here as well.
