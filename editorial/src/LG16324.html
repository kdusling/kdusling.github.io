Candidate resonant tetraneutron state populated by the 4He(8He,8Be) reaction
A candidate resonant tetraneutron state was found in the missing-mass spectrum obtained in the double-charge-exchange reaction $^4$He($^8$He,$^8$Be) at 186~MeV/u. The energy of the state is $0.83 \pm 0.65{\rm (stat.)} \pm1.25{\rm (sys.)}$~MeV above the threshold of four-neutron decay with a significance level of 4.9~$\sigma $. Utilizing the large positive $Q$-value of the ($^8$He,$^8$Be) reaction, an almost recoil-less condition of the four-neutron system was achieved so as to obtain weakly interacting four-neutron system efficiently.
