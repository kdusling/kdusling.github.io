Course Catalog Description

This is a basic course in statics. Topics include resultant of a force, equilibrium of forces, moments, couples, analysis of simple trusses, centroids, center of gravity, moments of inertia and friction.

Prerequisite(s): MTH 130, PHY 135; Corequisite: MET 201L

Course Objectives

  • Draw a free-body diagram of a rigid body under external forces.
  • Correctly analyze the forces acting on a body in two-dimensions.
  • Utilize cartesian coordinates and vectors to solve force problems.
  • Apply equilibrium equations to rigid bodies.
  • Apply the concepts of moment of a force and couples to problems.
  • Apply the laws of friction to applied problems.
  • Determine the forces in members of pin-connected Structures (trusses, frames and machines) using the methods of joints and sections.
  • Determine the Center of Gravity and Centroid of an area.
  • Determine the Moment of Inertia and radius of gyration of an area or mass.
