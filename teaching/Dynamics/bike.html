Determine the depth of the lake

In this problem the acceleration is a function of velocity. We can start with the relation $$ \frac{dv}{a}=dt $$ and integrate between initial/final velocity on the left hand side and initial/final time on the right hand side. $$ \begin{align} \int_{v_i}^{v} \frac{dv}{10-0.8v} &=& \int_{0}^{t} dt \\ \frac{1}{-0.8}\ln\left(\frac{10-0.8v}{10-0.8v_i}\right)&=& t \end{align}$$ $$ $$ See below for a tip on how the integration was done. Next we solve the above equation for $v$ $$ v = 12.5 - \left(12.5-v_i\right)e^{-0.8t} $$ We can now substitute the initial velocity of $v_i=16.5$ $$ v = 12.5 + 4e^{-0.8t} $$ We can now integrate to find $x$ as a function of $t$ $$ \int_0^x dx = \int_0^t v dt = \int_0^t \left( 12.5 + 4e^{-0.8t} \right) dt $$ $$ x = 12.5t - 5e^{-0.8t}+5 $$ At a time of $t=3$ we find $$ x=12.5(3) - 5e^{-0.8\times 3}+5=42.05~\ft $$
