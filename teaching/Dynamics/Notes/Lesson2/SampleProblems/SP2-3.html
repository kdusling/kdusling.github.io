Sample Problem 2-3
Airplane \(B\) is traveling at a constant velocity of 800 km/h, and is chasing airplane \(A\), which is traveling northeast at a constant velocity of 560 km/hr. At time \(t=0~\)s, airplane \(A\) is 640 km east of airplane \(B\). Determine (a) the direction airplane \(B\) should travel (measured from the east) to intercept plane \(A\) (b) how long it takes for airplane \(B\) to catch airplane \(A\) (c) the rate at which the distance between the planes is decreasing.

Both airplanes have a constant velocity. These velocity are

\begin{align*} \vec{v}_A&=\left(560\cos45\right)\hat{i}+\left(560\sin45\right)\hat{j}\\ \vec{v}_B&=\left(800\cos\theta\right)\hat{i}+\left(800\sin\theta\right)\hat{j} \end{align*} Integrating, the position is \begin{align*} \vec{r}_A&=\left[\left(560\cos45\right)t+640\right]\hat{i}+\left[\left(560\sin45\right)t\right]\hat{j}\\ \vec{r}_B&=\left[\left(800\cos\theta\right)t\right]\hat{i}+\left[\left(800\sin\theta\right)t\right]\hat{j} \end{align*}

Plane B intercepts plane A when \(\vec{r}_A=\vec{r_B}\). We first set the \(y-\)components equal and solve for \(\theta\).

\begin{align*} \left(800\sin\theta\right)t=\left(560\sin45\right)t \end{align*} The time, \(t\), cancels from both sides and we are left with \begin{align*} \sin\theta=\frac{\left(560\sin45\right)}{800}=0.4950\Longrightarrow \theta=29.67^\circ \end{align*}

Next we set the \(x-\)components equal and solve for \(t\):

\begin{align*} \left(560\cos45\right)t+640&=\left(800\cos29.67\right)t\\ 396t + 640&=695.1t\\ 640&=\left(695.1-396\right)t=299.1t\\ t&=\frac{640}{299.1}=2.14~\textrm{hr} \end{align*}

The rate at which the distance is decreasing is the magnitude of \(\vec{v}_{B/A}\). From above we have

\begin{align*} \vec{v}_A=396\hat{i}+396\hat{j}\\ \vec{v}_B=695.1\hat{i}+396\hat{j} \end{align*} \begin{align*} \vec{v}_{B/A}&=\vec{v}_B-\vec{v_A}=\left(695.1-396\right)\hat{i} + \left(396-396\right)\hat{j}=299.1\hat{i} \\ \vert \vec{v}_{B/A} \vert&= 299.1~\textrm{kph} \end{align*}
