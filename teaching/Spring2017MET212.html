Course Catalog Description

The objective of this course is to represent the basic principles of fluid mechanics and the application of those principles to practical, applied problems. Primary emphasis is on the topics of fluid statics, flow of fluids in piping systems, flow measurement, and forces developed by fluids in motion. The course is directed to anyone in a technical field where the ability to apply the principles of fluid mechanics is desirable.

Prerequisite(s): MTH 130, PHY 136

Course Objectives

  • Students will develop an understanding of the nature of fluids and their effectiveness in industrial applications.
  • Students will be able to calculate forces due to static fluid, viscosity, work and power generated by such fluids as well as losses in the system.
  • Students will be able to analyze and examine different systems through problem solving and designs of such fluid systems.

Class Policies

All assignments are individual effort unless the assignment has been specifically assigned as group work. There will be no make-ups for missed homeworks, quizzes and exams. No late homework submissions are accepted. No individual extra credit will be given.
